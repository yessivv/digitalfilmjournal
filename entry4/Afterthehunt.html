TUESDAY'S AT ANGELIKA DIGITAL JOURNAL



After The Hunt Directed by Luca Guadagnino is a crime/drama film starring Julia Roberts, Andrew Garfield, and Ayo Edebiri.


After The Hunt was quite an interesting watch since it spoke on many topics relating to prestige, tethered closely to the school I attend now. The film took place in Yale University with a student, Maggie, who acuses her male teacher, Henrik or Hank, of sexually assualting her. Maggie had a close relationship with her professer Alma. Early in the film Maggie is portrayed as being obssessed with Alma therefore the accusation felt false since Henrik and Alma had a close relationship. I loved that the film didn't write a narrative to simply villianize one character, instead I found myself understanding all narratives as nor wrong or right (Not defending Hank, as you watch you don't know who to trust). Growing up in Connecticut, Yale has always been captured in film as a high achieving University although in the film, Maggie plagarizes her essay, changing the perception that is often portrayed in the media. I felt it spoke alot on the prestige of schools and how common it is to see a facade, it has become difficult to believe a generation with such access to plagarism will continue to uphold the stature of these well-known schools.

White Business Men in a Room

When Maggie spoke to her professor, Alma, about being sexually assaulted, she said to keep it to herself since it would prevent her from finding a job in the future since men would be scared of 'touching her shoulder for too long'. The mention of jobs as a response to a traumatic situation sparked a multiude of thoughts in many unfair circumstances. Many women that experience a similar situation are forced to stay silent since they are then punished with the struggle to find a need for survival. The comment being made from a women really expands on how normalized workplace assualt has become.

Rory in Her Yale-ified Room

In Gilmore Girls, from the start of the series Rory is determined to attend Harvard, although due to her Grandfather's relationship with Yale University, she decides to attend Yale. After one discouraging comment, Rory then drops out of Yale. In Gossip Girl, Yale is often mentioned through various characters due to the prestige and Ivy title. After the Hunt almost normalized the feeling of attending Yale, instead of glorifying it, it almost undermined its reputation. Henrik being fired for sexually assualting a student speaks well on their morals but sparks a conversation of how this claim was started in the first place, from Maggie 'hanging out' with Alma and Henrik late at night.

The Food Trucks in New Haven

During the apartment scene interaction between Maggie and Alma, the setting reminded me of Long Wharf New Haven, this was a place I would drive past often with my mom and dad. I hadn't seen this place in a while until my cousin moved in with my mom and I. This summer we went to the food trucks along the water again. It brought me back to one of my favorite spots.
